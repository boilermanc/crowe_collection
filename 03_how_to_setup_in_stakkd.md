# How to Set Up Your Signal Chain in Stakkd (And What It Means)

Now that you understand what a signal chain is and why it matters, let's put that knowledge to work. The Stakkd app is designed to make managing your gear and understanding your signal path simple and intuitive. Here’s how to get started.

### Step 1: Add Your Gear to Stakkd

Before Stakkd can visualize your signal chain, it needs to know what you're working with. This is the foundation of your entire audio setup within the app.

1.  **Open the Stakkd Feature:** Navigate to the Stakkd section in your Rekkrd app.
2.  **Add a Component:** Tap the '+' or 'Add Gear' button.
3.  **Snap a Photo:** Use your phone’s camera to take a clear picture of your turntable, amplifier, preamp, or speakers. Our AI will do its best to identify the exact make and model.
4.  **Confirm or Enter Manually:** If the AI finds a match, confirm it. If not, you can easily search for and add your component manually.
5.  **Repeat for All Gear:** Do this for every component in your audio path, from the turntable to the speakers.

**Pro Tip:** The more gear you add, the more powerful Stakkd becomes. It will pull up specs, find manuals, and give you a complete picture of your audio rig.

### Step 2: Behold! Your Signal Chain, Visualized

Once you've added at least a source (like a turntable) and an output (like speakers), Stakkd automatically generates your signal chain visualization. You don't have to do anything else!

You will see a clear, top-to-bottom flow diagram showing each piece of gear connected in order. For a standard setup, it will look something like this:

1.  **Turntable** (e.g., Technics SL-1200MK7)
    *↓*
2.  **Phono Preamp** (e.g., Pro-Ject Phono Box S2)
    *↓*
3.  **Amplifier** (e.g., Yamaha A-S801)
    *↓*
4.  **Speakers** (e.g., KEF LS50 Meta)

### What This Visualization Tells You

This isn't just a pretty picture; it's a diagnostic tool. At a glance, you can answer critical questions about your setup:

*   **Is everything connected in the right order?** The diagram follows the actual path of the audio signal, making it easy to spot if something is out of place.
*   **Am I missing a crucial component?** If your turntable goes straight to an amplifier without a phono preamp in between (and your amp doesn't have a dedicated "PHONO" input), this diagram will make that gap obvious. This is the most common reason for poor sound in a new vinyl setup!
*   **What kind of signal is going where?** By tapping on each component, you can access its specifications. You can see that the turntable outputs a PHONO signal, which the PHONO PREAMP converts to a LINE signal, which the AMPLIFIER then boosts to a SPEAKER-LEVEL signal. Understanding these signal types is key to troubleshooting.

### Step 3: Drag, Drop, and Experiment

For most users, the standard signal chain is all you'll need. But for those with more complex setups—perhaps involving equalizers, effects units, or tape decks—Stakkd allows you to re-order your components.

Simply **press and hold** any component in your visualized chain, and then **drag it** to a new position. Stakkd will show you how this changes the flow of your audio.

**Warning:** For a standard vinyl setup, the Turntable → Phono Preamp → Amplifier → Speakers order is almost always correct. Only change this if you are an advanced user and know exactly why you are doing it!

### Putting It All Together

Your signal chain in Stakkd is your audio roadmap. It's a living document of your hi-fi system.

*   Use it to **learn** how your components work together.
*   Use it to **troubleshoot** problems when the sound isn't right.
*   Use it to **plan** your next upgrade by seeing where a new component would fit.

By combining the knowledge from our "Dummies" and "Deep Dive" guides with the practical visualization in Stakkd, you have everything you need to take control of your sound and ensure your vinyl collection is heard exactly as it was meant to be.
